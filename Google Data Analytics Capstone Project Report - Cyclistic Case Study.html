Introduction
This case study is the Capstone project for Google - Data Analytics Professional Certificate. The objective of this case study is to identify a marketing strategy for a fictional bike-share company, Cyclistic. The new marketing strategy is required to help the company convert its casual riders to subscribers.

Scenario
Lily Moreno (Director-Marketing), is looking for a new marketing strategy to convert casual riders into subscribers. Lily has asked the marketing analytics team to analyze past user data and find trends to help create the marketing strategy.

Stakeholders
Cyclistic - The bike-share company with more than 5800 bikes and 600 docking stations all over Chicago.
Lily Moreno - The director of marketing who has requested the analysis for the company’s new marketing strategy.
Cyclistic Marketing Analytics Team - A team of data analysts who are responsible for collecting, analyzing, and reporting data to define the market strategy.
Cyclistic Executive Team - The executive team who will review the recommended marketing program.

The business task
The new marketing strategy requires three components:
1. Behavioral differences between subscribers and casual riders,
2. Reasons why a casual rider would opt for Cyclistic annual memberships based on benefits,
3. Use of digital media to influence casual riders to becoming members.
The marketing analytics team has to analyse user data from four quarters of FY19 to identify trends and report results from their analysis.

Data sources
User data for the Financial year 2019-2020 (distributed in four quarters) has been identified, from among multiple data-sources provided for this case study. Each data set is in csv format and provides ride information about Cyclistic customers & users.

Tools used RStudio has been used for analysis and visualizations. This case study report is rendered in R Markdown format.

Conclusions:

Key takeaways:

  • Casual riders ride nearly 70% longer than members on an average.

  • Casual riders use the rides primarily for leisure while subscribers use the rides for commuting to workplaces.

  • Ride utilization is consistent across all days of the week for both casual riders and subscribers.

  • Casual riders do not use the service during the winter months as much as subscribers.

Recommendations:

  • Campaigns on promotions for annual memberships during the lean months to boost sales and to convert casual riders into annual subscribers.

  • Decrease in the price of one-way tickets and two-way tickets during work-days to encourage increase in casual riders during the week.

  • Increase in the price of one-way tickets and two-way tickets on weekends, so that casual riders switch to subscriptions.

  • Increase in bike-availability at starting-stations that have higher subscriber bookings on week days.

Addendum:

Additional input data that would be useful for further analysis:

  • Age and gender : to identify demographics of existing customers for planning promotional offers.

  • Block bookings : to identify strategies to target entire families or groups of riders based on their usage patterns.

  • Pricing plans : for cost benefit analysis and to identify campaigns to target specific rider-types.

  • Geographical data : for targeting potential customers from specific localities or districts based on rider patterns.

  • Ride-type data : to plan strategic locations for positioning docking stations and availability of alternative bike-types to ensure maximum utilization and subscription.
